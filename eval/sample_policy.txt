1. INTRODUCTION AND SCOPE
================================================================================

1.1 Purpose
This handbook consolidates policies governing employment, workplace conduct, information
security, and operational procedures at QABot Technologies GmbH. All employees, contractors,
interns, and temporary staff with access to QABot systems must comply with this document.

1.2 Applicability
Policies apply to all business units including Engineering, Product, Sales, Customer Success,
Finance, Legal, and People Operations. Regional supplements may apply for employees based in
Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and other EEA locations.

1.3 Definitions
"Employee" means any individual with an active employment or contractor agreement.
"Confidential Information" means data classified as Internal, Restricted, or Highly Restricted.
"Production Systems" means customer-facing services, internal platforms with SLA commitments,
and documentation portals serving external users.
"Hybrid Employee" means an employee splitting work between office and remote locations.

1.4 Acknowledgment
All employees must acknowledge receipt of this handbook within five business days of hire or
within ten business days after a material policy update. Acknowledgment is recorded in the HRIS.

1.5 Questions and Exceptions
Policy questions should be directed to People Operations at peopleops@qabot.example.
Exception requests require written approval from the employee's director and, where applicable,
Information Security or Legal.

2. REMOTE WORK AND HYBRID WORKING POLICY
================================================================================

2.1 Overview
QABot supports flexible hybrid work to attract talent, reduce commute burden, and maintain
collaboration in our Böblingen headquarters and satellite offices. This section defines
eligibility, approval workflows, availability expectations, and security requirements.

2.2 Eligibility
All full-time employees who have completed probation (typically 6 months) are eligible for
hybrid arrangements unless role requirements mandate on-site presence (e.g., data center
technicians, reception, certain lab roles). Part-time employees may request hybrid work;
approval is at manager discretion.

2.3 Remote Day Allowance
All employees may work remotely up to three days per week. Remote work must be approved by
the direct manager. Managers document approved patterns in the team roster. Exceeding three
remote days requires vice-president approval and a business justification.

2.4 Core Hours and Availability
On remote days, employees must be available between 10:00 and 16:00 Central European Time
for meetings, pair programming, customer calls, and synchronous collaboration. Outside core
hours, async communication via Slack and email is expected with reasonable response times.

2.5 Office Anchor Days
Each engineering team designates at least one "anchor day" per week for in-person collaboration.
Team leads publish anchor days at the start of each quarter. Attendance on anchor days is
mandatory unless approved absence is recorded in the calendar.

2.6 Equipment and Workspace
The company provides a laptop and an external monitor for eligible remote employees. Standard
issue laptops are refreshed every 36 months. Employees are responsible for a stable internet
connection with minimum 50 Mbps download and 10 Mbps upload. Home office ergonomic assessments
are available upon request through People Operations.

2.7 Security Requirements for Remote Work
All remote access must use the company VPN. Confidential documents must not be stored on
personal devices, USB drives, or unsanctioned cloud storage. Screen locks must activate after
five minutes of inactivity. Video calls discussing Restricted data require approved backgrounds
or blur filters.

2.8 International Remote Work
Working from outside the employee's contracted country for more than 14 consecutive days
requires advance approval from HR and Legal due to tax and labor law implications. Emergency
exceptions may be granted for maximum 30 days per calendar year.

2.9 Performance Expectations
Remote work is not a substitute for performance standards. Managers evaluate outcomes, not
physical presence. Persistent failure to meet availability or deliverable expectations may
result in revocation of remote privileges.

2.10 Review
This policy is reviewed annually by the People Operations team. Feedback may be submitted via
the internal policy portal.

3. INFORMATION SECURITY AND ACCEPTABLE USE
================================================================================

3.1 Security Principles
QABot follows defense-in-depth: least privilege, separation of duties, encryption in transit
and at rest, and continuous monitoring. All employees share responsibility for protecting
customer data and intellectual property.

3.2 Acceptable Use of Company Systems
Company laptops, networks, email, and SaaS tools are provided for business purposes.
Limited personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work, violate law, or consume
excessive bandwidth. Prohibited activities include unauthorized cryptocurrency mining, torrenting
copyrighted material, and accessing adult or extremist content on company networks.

3.3 Software Installation
Only software from the approved catalog or installed via the corporate MDM may be used on
company devices. Requests for exceptions go through the Service Desk with manager approval.

3.4 Email and Phishing
Employees must report suspected phishing to security@qabot.example within one hour. Never
click suspicious links. Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for email and SSO.

3.5 Removable Media
Use of USB storage devices is disabled by default. Exceptions require Information Security
approval and device encryption.

3.6 Clean Desk and Clear Screen
In office environments, confidential papers must be secured when unattended. Whiteboards with
customer data must be erased after meetings.

3.7 Security Awareness Training
All employees complete annual security training and quarterly phishing simulations. Failure to
complete training by due date may suspend VPN access until completion.

4. IDENTITY, ACCESS MANAGEMENT, AND AUTHENTICATION
================================================================================

4.1 Single Sign-On
Corporate applications authenticate via the company SSO provider. Password reuse across
personal and corporate accounts is prohibited.

4.2 Password Standards
Passwords must be at least 14 characters and include uppercase, lowercase, numeric, and
special characters. Password managers (1Password Business) are provided to all employees.

4.3 Multi-Factor Authentication
MFA is required for VPN, source code repositories, production consoles, and HR systems.
Hardware security keys are issued to employees with production access.

4.4 Access Provisioning
New hire access is provisioned via automated workflows tied to HRIS start dates. Managers
approve role-based access requests in the IAM portal. Access reviews occur quarterly.

4.5 Offboarding
Upon termination, all access is revoked within four hours for involuntary separation and
within 24 hours for voluntary resignation. Laptops must be returned within five business days.

4.6 Privileged Access
Administrative accounts are separate from daily-use accounts. Privileged sessions are logged
and reviewed monthly.

5. DATA CLASSIFICATION, PRIVACY, AND RETENTION
================================================================================

5.1 Classification Levels
Public: marketing materials approved for external release.
Internal: operational documents not intended for public disclosure.
Restricted: customer data, contracts, unreleased product plans.
Highly Restricted: credentials, encryption keys, M&A materials, regulated health/financial data.

5.2 Handling Requirements
Restricted and Highly Restricted data must be encrypted at rest, shared only on approved
platforms, and labeled in document headers. Downloading Restricted data to local devices
requires business justification.

5.3 GDPR and Privacy
Personal data processing follows GDPR principles: lawfulness, purpose limitation, data
minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, and confidentiality. Data Subject
Access Requests are handled by Legal within 30 days.

5.4 Retention Schedule
Customer contracts and billing records: retained for 7 years after contract end.
Employee personnel files: retained for 10 years after separation.
Security incident records: retained for 7 years.
Ephemeral chat logs in Slack: 365 days unless legal hold applies.

5.5 Data Loss Prevention
DLP rules scan email and cloud storage for patterns matching credit cards, API keys, and
national ID numbers. Violations trigger automatic quarantine and Security review.

5.6 Cross-Border Transfers
Transfers outside the EEA require Standard Contractual Clauses or adequacy decisions documented
by Legal.

6. INCIDENT RESPONSE AND BUSINESS CONTINUITY
================================================================================

6.1 Incident Categories
Severity 1 (Critical): production outage affecting all customers.
Severity 2 (Major): partial outage or data exposure risk.
Severity 3 (Minor): isolated team impact.
Severity 4 (Informational): near-miss or policy violation without customer impact.

6.2 Reporting Obligations
All employees must report suspected security incidents to security@qabot.example within
24 hours of discovery. Severity 1 incidents require immediate phone escalation to the on-call
Security lead via PagerDuty.

6.3 Incident Response Team
The IRT includes representatives from Security, Engineering, Legal, Communications, and
Customer Success. The incident commander rotates weekly among senior engineers.

6.4 Containment and Eradication
Containment steps may include isolating affected systems, rotating credentials, and blocking
malicious IPs. Root cause analysis must be completed within 10 business days for Severity 1–2.

6.5 Customer Notification
Legal and Communications jointly determine customer notification timelines for data breaches
under GDPR (72-hour authority notification where applicable).

6.6 Business Continuity
Critical systems target RPO of 1 hour and RTO of 4 hours. Annual tabletop exercises validate
failover to secondary EU regions.

6.7 Post-Incident Review
Blameless postmortems are published internally within five business days. Action items tracked
in Jira with owners and due dates.

7. HUMAN RESOURCES — LEAVE, BENEFITS, AND CONDUCT
================================================================================

7.1 Working Hours
Standard full-time schedule is 40 hours per week. Overtime for non-exempt roles follows local
labor law. Exempt employees are expected to manage time flexibly while meeting objectives.

7.2 Paid Time Off (PTO)
Employees accrue 30 days PTO per year in Germany (including public holidays per local calendar).
Up to 5 days unused PTO may carry over to the next calendar year; excess is forfeited unless
director approval is granted.

7.3 Sick Leave
Employees notify their manager before 09:30 on sick days. Medical certificates are required
for absences exceeding three consecutive days.

7.4 Parental Leave
Parental leave follows statutory requirements in the employee's country. HR coordinates
documentation eight weeks before expected leave start.

7.5 Benefits
QABot offers Deutschland job-ticket subsidy, bike leasing, EGYM Wellpass, private health
supplement (country-dependent), pension contributions, and learning stipend of 2,000 EUR per year.

7.6 Code of Conduct
Harassment, discrimination, retaliation, and violence are zero-tolerance violations. Reports
may be made to HR or the anonymous ethics hotline.

7.7 Performance Management
Annual performance reviews occur in Q4. Mid-year check-ins are mandatory. Performance improvement
plans last 60 days with weekly manager syncs.

7.8 Remote Work and HR
Hybrid eligibility is described in Section 2. HRIS records approved remote patterns. Changes
require manager update within two business days.

8. IT SERVICE MANAGEMENT AND END-USER SUPPORT
================================================================================

8.1 Service Desk
For IT issues during remote work, contact helpdesk@qabot.example or extension 4500. Hours:
Monday–Friday 08:00–18:00 CET. Severity 1 production issues: 24/7 on-call via PagerDuty.

8.2 Ticketing
All requests must be logged in ServiceNow. Slack DMs to IT staff do not replace tickets for
trackable work.

8.3 Standard Lead Times
Laptop provisioning: 5 business days for new hires.
Access requests: 2 business days after manager approval.
VPN troubleshooting: same business day target.

8.4 Change Management
Production changes follow CAB approval except break-glass emergencies documented within
one hour post-change.

8.5 Documentation Platforms
Internal technical documentation is published via the Eclipse-based online help system and
the developer portal. Documentation updates are built and released through Jenkins CI pipelines
described in Section 10.

8.6 Endpoint Management
Devices enroll in MDM at first boot. Lost or stolen devices must be reported within two hours
to trigger remote wipe.

9. TRAVEL, EXPENSES, AND PROCUREMENT
================================================================================

9.1 Business Travel
Travel must be pre-approved in Concur for trips exceeding 500 EUR estimated cost. Book via
approved travel agency where corporate rates apply.

9.2 Expense Reimbursement
Submit expenses within 30 days of incurring cost. Receipts required above 25 EUR. Alcohol is
not reimbursable except approved client entertainment with director sign-off.

9.3 Per Diems
Domestic Germany per diem follows BMF rates. International per diems use company table updated
quarterly.

9.4 Procurement
Purchases above 5,000 EUR require competitive quotes. Software purchases require Security review
for data processing agreements.

9.5 Corporate Card
Issued to managers and frequent travelers. Personal charges are grounds for card revocation.

10. ENGINEERING STANDARDS AND SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT LIFECYCLE
================================================================================

10.1 Source Control
All production code resides in GitHub Enterprise. Direct commits to main are prohibited.
Pull requests require one approval for internal tools and two for customer-facing services.

10.2 Continuous Integration
Jenkins CI runs unit tests, integration tests, static analysis, and documentation builds on
every pull request. Main branch must remain green. Failed builds block release.

10.3 Testing Requirements
Unit test coverage target: 80% for new code in core services. End-to-end tests run nightly
against staging environments.

10.4 Release Management
Releases use semantic versioning. Customer-facing releases require release notes, rollback plan,
and on-call handoff. Maintenance windows communicated 72 hours in advance.

10.5 Documentation as Code
Technical writers and engineers author XML source files transformed via XSLT into HTML for the
online help system. Documentation PRs follow the same review process as code.

10.6 Observability
Services export metrics to Prometheus, traces to Tempo, and logs to Loki. SLO error budgets
 reviewed weekly in engineering staff meeting.

10.7 Dependency Management
Dependabot alerts must be triaged within 7 days. Critical CVEs patched within 48 hours for
production dependencies.

11. AI SYSTEMS, LLM USAGE, AND RETRIEVAL-AUGMENTED DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

11.1 Approved AI Tools
Employees may use company-approved LLM integrations for coding assistance, documentation
drafting, and internal search. Uploading Restricted or Highly Restricted data to public LLM
services is prohibited.

11.2 RAG Architecture Standards
Internal knowledge systems use retrieval-augmented generation pipelines: document ingestion,
chunking (default 512 tokens with 128 overlap), embedding, vector storage, retrieval, and
grounded generation. Answers must cite source documents.

11.3 Evaluation Metrics
Production RAG systems must track retrieval hit rate, answer groundedness, latency (p50/p95),
and hallucination rate on golden question sets reviewed quarterly.

11.4 Model Selection
Open-source models (e.g., Llama, Qwen) may be deployed on-premises for air-gapped workloads.
Cloud models require vendor DPAs and EU data residency review.

11.5 Human Oversight
Customer-facing AI features require human review workflows for high-risk outputs. Escalation
paths documented in runbooks.

11.6 Prompt Engineering
System prompts must instruct models to answer only from retrieved context and refuse when
evidence is insufficient. Prompt versions are stored in Git with change history.

11.7 Vector Databases
Approved vector stores include Chroma (development), managed Pinecone (staging), and internal
Milvus clusters (production). Collection naming follows env-team-corpus pattern.

11.8 MCP and Tool Integration
Model Context Protocol servers exposing search and ticket creation must authenticate via SSO
and respect row-level security on source systems.
